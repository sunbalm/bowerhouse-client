How to Play

4-Player Euchre

BowerHouse starts with classic 4-player euchre. Seats 1 and 3 are partners. Seats 2 and 4 are partners.

Trump

Players first decide whether to order up the visible up card. If everyone passes, players may name any other suit as trump. The dealer must choose if everyone else passes.

Bowers

The right bower is the jack of trump. The left bower is the jack of the same color suit. Both count as trump.

Playing

You must follow suit when possible. If you cannot follow suit, you may play any card. Highest trump wins. If no trump is played, highest card of the led suit wins.

Scoring

The maker team needs at least 3 tricks. Making it scores 1 point. Taking all 5 tricks scores 2 points. Getting euchred gives the defending team 2 points. First team to 10 wins.

Timers

If a player runs out of time or disconnects, BowerHouse will auto-play for them so the game can continue.

2-Player Euchre

Two-player euchre is head-to-head. Each player has 4 cards in hand, 4 face-up table cards, and 4 face-down cards underneath those table cards.

Trump

The non-dealer chooses trump first or passes. If they pass, the dealer must name trump.

Playing Table Cards

You may play from your hand or from your own face-up table cards. When a face-up table card is played, the face-down card underneath it is revealed.

Scoring

The maker needs at least 7 of the 12 tricks. Taking 7 or more tricks scores 1 point. Taking all 12 tricks scores 2 points. If the maker gets fewer than 7 tricks, the defender scores 2 points. First to 10 wins.
